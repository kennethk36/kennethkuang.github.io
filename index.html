Project 01

Space Rover

Competition-ready rover with a custom SolidWorks chassis designed for Mars-analog terrain — built for the University Rover Challenge.

Mechanical Design SEDS SolidWorks FEA

Problem / Goal

Design a lightweight but structurally sound rover chassis capable of navigating Mars-analog terrain for a university competition. The system had to meet strict weight and cost constraints while surviving rough outdoor conditions.

Design & Implementation

  • Modeled the full chassis frame and drivetrain assembly in SolidWorks, optimizing for torsional rigidity under uneven loading
  • Ran finite element analysis (FEA) to identify stress concentrations and iteratively redesigned critical joint geometry
  • Designed modular wheel mounts for rapid field replacement during competition rounds
  • Coordinated vendor quotes for aluminum extrusion and 3D-printed components to stay within build budget

Challenges

  • Balancing aggressive weight reduction with structural integrity under dynamic terrain loads
  • Material sourcing within competition budget constraints — required evaluating 6+ vendors
  • Coordinating design change reviews across a multi-person team with varying CAD experience levels

Results

Chassis design completed and reviewed by the full engineering team. Currently in fabrication phase ahead of competition deadline.

Project 02

FPV Drone

Custom-built FPV racing drone with a 3D-printed frame designed from scratch — optimized for weight distribution and stable flight.

Hardware 3D Printing Robotics Club CAD Electronics

Problem / Goal

Design and fabricate a functional FPV drone from the ground up — no kit, custom frame. The goal was to understand the full hardware stack from frame geometry to ESC tuning, while building a platform for the Robotics Club to demonstrate and teach with.

Design & Implementation

  • Designed a custom frame in CAD, iterating arm length and motor spacing to optimize center-of-mass and weight distribution
  • 3D-printed frame prototypes in PLA, tested under load, then upgraded to PETG for impact resistance
  • Assembled flight electronics: F4 flight controller, 4-in-1 ESC stack, brushless motors, VTX, and FPV camera
  • Tuned Betaflight PID settings across multiple test flights to achieve stable hover and responsive control

Challenges

  • Vibration resonance in early frame designs causing gyro noise — resolved by adding damping standoffs under the FC stack
  • ESC desync issues under throttle bursts — diagnosed and fixed via BlHeli ESC configuration adjustments
  • Weight creep across iterations required going back to CAD to eliminate material from non-structural areas

Results

Achieved stable, controllable flight on the final build. Drone used as a club demonstration platform and reference design for new members learning hardware assembly.

Project 03

Turret Defense System

Designed and built an automated turret defense system with real-time target tracking — integrating servo-driven pan/tilt mechanics, embedded microcontroller logic, and a computer vision pipeline into a fully autonomous fire-control loop.

Mechanical Design Embedded Systems Computer Vision Servo Control

Problem / Goal

Build a fully autonomous turret system capable of detecting, tracking, and engaging moving targets without human input. The goal was to close the entire detection-to-engagement loop in hardware — from real-time image processing through mechanical actuation — while keeping latency low enough for practical tracking performance.

System Overview

The system is structured as three tightly coupled layers: perception, control, and actuation.

  • Perception: camera feed processed through a computer vision pipeline for target detection and centroid estimation
  • Control: PID-based feedback loop on pan and tilt axes, running on an embedded microcontroller
  • Actuation: dual-servo pan/tilt mechanism designed and fabricated to mount the camera and firing mechanism as a single rigid assembly

Design & Implementation

  • Designed the pan/tilt bracket geometry to minimize moment arm and reduce servo load during rapid slews
  • Implemented target detection using color/shape thresholding and contour analysis to extract target centroid in real time
  • Tuned PID gains for both axes independently — balancing tracking speed against overshoot and oscillation
  • Integrated firing actuation trigger logic with a deadband zone to prevent false engagements on edge detections
  • Iterated on mount stiffness after observing resonance feedback between servo motion and camera image stability

Challenges

  • Latency in the vision-to-servo loop caused tracking lag — resolved by optimizing frame processing and reducing resolution on the detection pass
  • Mechanical resonance between the servo mounts and camera introduced oscillation into the PID loop — fixed with stiffer bracket geometry
  • Distinguishing target from background clutter required iterating on detection thresholds across varying lighting conditions

Results

Achieved reliable real-time tracking of moving targets with sub-100ms latency from detection to servo response. The system successfully engaged targets autonomously across a range of speeds and trajectories, validating the full closed-loop pipeline from perception through actuation.
